# design-team-health-checklist

> Team health indicators and warning signs for design organizations, based on Dave Malouf's Three Lenses framework and scaling principles.

## Overview

This checklist helps identify team health issues before they become critical. Use it monthly or quarterly to catch problems early, especially during periods of growth or change.

## How to Use

1. Review each section with your leadership team
2. Check items that are TRUE warnings (not aspirational)
3. Any checked warning sign requires investigation
4. Use the "What to Do" guidance for immediate action

---

## HOW WE WORK - Health Indicators

### Workflow Health

**Green Flags (Healthy)**
- [ ] Projects complete on or near estimated timelines
- [ ] Designers know what to work on and why
- [ ] Reviews happen on schedule with constructive feedback
- [ ] Handoffs are smooth with few questions
- [ ] Documentation is up-to-date and useful

**Yellow Flags (Warning)**
- [ ] Some projects consistently run over timeline
- [ ] Occasional confusion about priorities
- [ ] Reviews sometimes delayed or skipped
- [ ] Handoffs require follow-up clarification
- [ ] Documentation becoming outdated

**Red Flags (Critical)**
- [ ] Most projects significantly delayed
- [ ] Designers unclear on priorities
- [ ] Reviews rarely happen or are combative
- [ ] Handoffs frequently rejected or cause rework
- [ ] No one trusts documentation

**What to Do:**
| Warning Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Yellow | Investigate root causes, add process checkpoints |
| Red | Stop and fix processes before continuing work |

---

### Tool Health

**Green Flags (Healthy)**
- [ ] High adoption of standard tools (>90%)
- [ ] Tools support the workflow well
- [ ] Design system actively used (>80% component usage)
- [ ] Integrations working smoothly
- [ ] Team knows how to use tools effectively

**Yellow Flags (Warning)**
- [ ] Some designers using non-standard tools
- [ ] Workarounds needed for tool limitations
- [ ] Design system usage declining
- [ ] Integration issues causing friction
- [ ] Tool training gaps emerging

**Red Flags (Critical)**
- [ ] Tool fragmentation across team
- [ ] Major workflow gaps due to tools
- [ ] Design system abandoned or ignored
- [ ] Integrations broken
- [ ] Team frustrated with tools

**What to Do:**
| Warning Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Yellow | Survey team, identify specific issues, plan fixes |
| Red | Conduct tool audit (see `design-tooling-audit.md`) |

---

### Collaboration Health

**Green Flags (Healthy)**
- [ ] Regular touchpoints with eng/PM/stakeholders
- [ ] Cross-functional partners satisfied with design
- [ ] Feedback is timely and constructive
- [ ] Decisions happen efficiently
- [ ] Trust between design and partners

**Yellow Flags (Warning)**
- [ ] Some friction with specific teams
- [ ] Occasional delayed feedback
- [ ] Decisions sometimes unclear
- [ ] Some relationship strain

**Red Flags (Critical)**
- [ ] Design seen as bottleneck
- [ ] Chronic conflict with partners
- [ ] Feedback loops broken
- [ ] Decisions circumvent design
- [ ] Trust has broken down

**What to Do:**
| Warning Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Yellow | Strengthen rituals, improve communication |
| Red | Stakeholder reset meetings, rebuild relationships |

## HOW WE GROW - Health Indicators

### Hiring Health

**Green Flags (Healthy)**
- [ ] Attracting qualified candidates
- [ ] Interview process efficient (<45 days)
- [ ] High offer acceptance rate (>80%)
- [ ] Diverse candidate pipeline
- [ ] Positive candidate experience

**Yellow Flags (Warning)**
- [ ] Candidate quality declining
- [ ] Hiring taking longer (45-60 days)
- [ ] Some offer rejections
- [ ] Diversity gaps emerging
- [ ] Some candidate complaints

**Red Flags (Critical)**
- [ ] Can't find qualified candidates
- [ ] Hiring significantly delayed (>60 days)
- [ ] Most offers rejected
- [ ] No diversity in candidates
- [ ] Poor employer reputation

**What to Do:**
| Warning Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Yellow | Review employer brand, optimize interview process |
| Red | Major recruiting strategy overhaul needed |

---

### Onboarding Health

**Green Flags (Healthy)**
- [ ] New hires productive within 90 days
- [ ] High onboarding satisfaction
- [ ] New hires feel welcomed
- [ ] Low early attrition (<10% in first year)
- [ ] New hires become contributors

**Yellow Flags (Warning)**
- [ ] Some new hires struggling longer
- [ ] Mixed onboarding feedback
- [ ] Integration taking longer
- [ ] Some early departures
- [ ] New hires seem lost

**Red Flags (Critical)**
- [ ] New hires not becoming productive
- [ ] Poor onboarding experience
- [ ] New hires feel unsupported
- [ ] High early attrition (>20%)
- [ ] "Sink or swim" culture

**What to Do:**
| Warning Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Yellow | Gather feedback, strengthen program |
| Red | Redesign onboarding program immediately |

---

### Career Development Health

**Green Flags (Healthy)**
- [ ] Clear career paths understood
- [ ] Regular growth conversations
- [ ] Internal promotions common (>50%)
- [ ] Skills development happening
- [ ] Career satisfaction high

**Yellow Flags (Warning)**
- [ ] Career path clarity declining
- [ ] Growth conversations inconsistent
- [ ] Some external hiring for senior roles
- [ ] Learning happening sporadically
- [ ] Some career frustration

**Red Flags (Critical)**
- [ ] No clear career paths
- [ ] No growth conversations
- [ ] Most senior roles filled externally
- [ ] No learning or development
- [ ] Career frustration widespread

**What to Do:**
| Warning Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Yellow | Reinforce career ladder, train managers |
| Red | Build or rebuild career framework |

---

### Retention Health

**Green Flags (Healthy)**
- [ ] High retention (>85% annual)
- [ ] Departures are for positive reasons
- [ ] Exit feedback is constructive
- [ ] Strong alumni network
- [ ] Team stability

**Yellow Flags (Warning)**
- [ ] Retention starting to decline (75-85%)
- [ ] Some unexpected departures
- [ ] Exit interviews show patterns
- [ ] Key people considering leaving
- [ ] Some instability

**Red Flags (Critical)**
- [ ] High turnover (<75% annual)
- [ ] Critical people leaving
- [ ] Exit interviews show systemic issues
- [ ] Recruitment can't keep up
- [ ] Team in flux

**What to Do:**
| Warning Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Yellow | Stay interviews, address feedback |
| Red | Emergency retention actions, root cause analysis |

## SCALING-SPECIFIC WARNING SIGNS

### Warning Signs During Growth (5-15 designers)

- [ ] Original processes no longer scaling
- [ ] Tribal knowledge becoming problematic
- [ ] Culture starting to fragment
- [ ] Inconsistent practices emerging
- [ ] First managers struggling

**What to Do:** Formalize processes, document everything, invest in management

### Warning Signs During Scaling (15-50 designers)

- [ ] Communication breaking down
- [ ] Duplicated efforts across teams
- [ ] Quality becoming inconsistent
- [ ] Managers overwhelmed
- [ ] Design system fragmentation

**What to Do:** Add coordination roles, strengthen governance, scale rituals

### Warning Signs at Scale (50+ designers)

- [ ] Bureaucracy creeping in
- [ ] Innovation slowing
- [ ] Top talent leaving for smaller orgs
- [ ] Local teams diverging
- [ ] Global alignment failing

**What to Do:** Reduce overhead, increase autonomy, simplify processes

## QUICK REFERENCE: CRITICAL WARNING SIGNS

These always require immediate attention:

| Category | Critical Warning | Immediate Action |
|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| Retention | Key people leaving | Stay interviews, retention offers |
| Well-being | Burnout cases | Reduce load immediately |
| Culture | Psychological safety failing | Leadership intervention |
| Quality | Consistent failures | Stop and fix processes |
| Collaboration | Trust breakdown | Relationship reset |

---

## NOTES

> "Culture is fragile at scale - intentionality is required." - Dave Malouf

Use this checklist:
- Monthly for growing teams
- Quarterly for stable teams
- After any major change (reorg, leadership change, etc.)
- When something "feels off"

Prevention is easier than repair. Catch yellow flags before they turn red.
